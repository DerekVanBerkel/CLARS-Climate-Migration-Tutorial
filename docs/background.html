Background: Climate Migration in the Great Lakes & Lake Victoria

üåç CLARS: Understanding and Adapting to Climate Migration in the Great Lakes and Lake Victoria Regions

CLARS (Climate Adaptation and Resilience Strategies) is an international research project (2024-2027) focused on developing co-produced strategies to reduce socio-economic vulnerabilities for climate migrants and host communities in the Lake Victoria Basin and Great Lakes Region, aiming to build resilience through interdisciplinary research, knowledge exchange, and collaboration between academia, government, and communities. Led by institutions like McMaster University, University of Michigan, and the Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research, it uses ‚Äúliving labs‚Äù in cities like Kampala and Michigan to create inclusive, place-based solutions for climate-induced migration challenges

üå¶Ô∏è Why Climate Migration Matters

Climate change is reshaping where people live and work.
Global estimates suggest that by 2050, between 200 and 216 million people may relocate within their countries due to environmental changes such as droughts, sea-level rise, and reduced agricultural productivity.
By 2100, that number could climb to 2 billion.

Sudden climate shocks‚Äîfloods, storms, and wildfires‚Äîare also displacing people. In 2022, there were over 32 million disaster-related displacements, mostly due to weather-related hazards.

While much research focuses on why people move, CLARS also examines what happens next‚Äîexploring the social, environmental, and planning challenges in receiving communities.

üåê What Makes CLARS Different

CLARS focuses on the destinations of climate migration, not just its origins. It treats migration as a dynamic process and studies how local planning can support both newcomers and existing residents under stress.

By comparing two major freshwater regions‚Äîthe Lake Victoria Basin (LVB) in East Africa and the Great Lakes Region (GLR) in North America‚ÄîCLARS facilitates cross-regional learning between Global South and Global North cities.

üéØ CLARS Goals

CLARS focuses on five urban sites:
Kampala (Uganda), Mwanza (Tanzania), Eldoret (Kenya) and surrounding areas (e.g., Kisii), and Detroit (USA).

üîç Key Research Questions

  1. Participation: How can PPGIS (Public Participation GIS) broaden who participates in migration planning and ensure diverse voices are included?
  2. Co-production: Can co-produced tools like PIVOT help develop better solutions for managing climate migration impacts for both migrants and vulnerable residents?
  3. Capacity: Can shared knowledge increase capacity to assess climate in-migration impacts on:
    • Environment (e.g., land use, green infrastructure)
    • Justice (e.g., who benefits or bears the burden)
    • Livelihoods (e.g., jobs, services, social networks)
  4. Scaling: What factors support the scaling and uptake of co-produced tools across regions?

üåç Why Compare Great Lakes & Lake Victoria Regions?

Both regions face climate stress, urbanization, and inequality:

  • In the Lake Victoria Basin, migration driven by flooding, rainfall shifts, and farming change is already transforming cities.
  • In the Great Lakes Region, climate-induced migration is expected to grow as people seek freshwater, safety, and climate stability.

The LVB offers experience with climate-related mobility.
The GLR offers innovations in planning and governance.

Together, they can co-develop insights for equitable and sustainable adaptation.
